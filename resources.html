Scholarships

Islamic Scholarship Fund

The Islamic Scholarship Fund provides multiple scholarships for Muslim students studying in policy, media, law, and film related majors. The scholarship application opens in November and remains open until March. The scholarship award amounts range from $3,000-$10,000.

The Islamic Society of North America

The Islamic Society of North America hosts multiple scholarships. The scholarship applications are due on June 30th, and award amounts range from $500-$2500. Among the scholarships awarded is the Dr. Abdulmunim A. Shakir Scholarship, which awards 20 college freshmen in any field of study $1,000.

The Zakat Foundation of America

The Zakat foundation of America is offering $30,000 to American Muslims who are dedicated to community service. The scholarship application is due on May 15th. The scholarship is open to high-school seniors who reside in CA, DE, MD, VA, FL, IL, NC, NJ, NY, PA, TX, MI, GA, or OH.

Financial Tips

The 50/30/20 Budgeting Golden Rule

The 50/30/20 Rule was popularized by the U.S. Senator Elizabeth Warren in her book: All Your Worth: The Ultimate Lifetime Money Plan. The rule states that 50% of your after-tax income should be spent on needs. The remaining half of the income should be split 30/20, with 30% of your income spent on your wants and the remaining 20% on savings and investments.
